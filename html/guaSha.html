Gua Sha Therapy

Traditional wisdom meets modern relief through gentle, restorative care.

Gua Sha is a time-honored manual therapy rooted in Traditional Chinese Medicine that involves gently scraping the skin with a smooth-edged tool—typically made of jade, horn, ceramic, or stone—to promote circulation, release stagnation, and encourage the body’s natural healing processes.

Despite its ancient origins, Gua Sha has gained increasing recognition in modern integrative medicine for its therapeutic benefits in musculoskeletal pain, inflammation, tension, and immune regulation. The technique works by stimulating the surface of the skin and underlying fascia to increase microcirculation, break up myofascial adhesions, and enhance lymphatic drainage.

What Is Gua Sha Used For?

  • Neck, shoulder, and back pain
  • Chronic muscle tightness and tension
  • Headaches and migraines
  • TMJ dysfunction
  • Respiratory conditions such as cough, asthma, and chest congestion
  • Poor circulation or lymphatic stagnation
  • Immune support during viral or seasonal illness
  • Facial rejuvenation (in cosmetic applications, with a lighter touch)

How Gua Sha Helps

  • Relieves muscular tension and improves range of motion
  • Stimulates microcirculation and oxygenation of soft tissues
  • Promotes detoxification and lymphatic flow
  • Modulates the immune response, aiding in the recovery from colds or chronic inflammation
  • Activates the parasympathetic nervous system, supporting relaxation and stress relief
  • Enhances absorption of topical herbal oils and formulas used during treatment

What to Expect

Gua Sha is a gentle but deeply effective treatment. While some mild redness or petechiae (tiny red spots) may appear after the session, this is a natural response and typically resolves within a few days. Most patients describe the treatment as soothing, relieving, and deeply satisfying, especially when tension or stagnation is present.

In my practice, Gua Sha is often integrated into broader treatment sessions—such as acupuncture, cupping, or herbal therapy—to create a multidimensional therapeutic experience that supports healing from every angle.
