Cupping Therapy

Ancient technique. Modern results. Deep relief starts here.

Cupping therapy is a traditional technique used across many cultures—most notably within Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM)—in which glass, silicone, or bamboo cups are applied to the skin to create suction. This gentle vacuum draws the skin and superficial muscle layer into the cup, promoting blood flow, loosening fascia, and facilitating detoxification through the lymphatic system.

Far from being just a trend, cupping has been validated by modern research for its role in pain reduction, inflammation modulation, and muscular recovery, making it a valuable adjunct to both traditional and contemporary medical care.

What Is Cupping Used For?

  • Chronic and acute musculoskeletal pain (neck, back, shoulders, hips)
  • Sports recovery and muscle fatigue
  • Tension headaches and migraines
  • Respiratory issues like asthma, cough, or bronchitis
  • Digestive sluggishness or bloating
  • Lymphatic congestion and water retention
  • Stress-related tension and emotional stagnation
  • Scar tissue softening and post-injury rehabilitation

How Cupping Helps

  • Increases local circulation to relieve stagnation and nourish tissues
  • Releases fascia and eases muscular adhesions
  • Stimulates lymphatic drainage and supports detoxification
  • Reduces inflammation by enhancing oxygenation and waste clearance
  • Promotes deep relaxation, helping to reset the nervous system
  • Accelerates healing from injury, overuse, or post-surgical stiffness

What to Expect

Cupping therapy is typically painless and deeply relaxing. You may feel a pulling or stretching sensation in the targeted area as circulation is stimulated. After treatment, it’s common to see temporary round marks or discoloration on the skin—these are not bruises, but signs of stagnation being released and processed by the body. These marks fade naturally within a few days.
